What is Trauma Recovery

The gradual restoration of safety, flexibility, agency, and connection in a nervous system that has learned to survive without them.

Primary Goal
Restore Nervous System Safety
Reduce hypervigilance and collapse states through predictability, rhythm, and containment.
Primary Goal
Rebuild Circadian Rhythm
Anchor sleep/wake times. Morning sunlight. Consistent mealtimes. Reduce light disruption.
Primary Goal
Repair Metabolic Damage
Address inflammation, cortisol and glucose variability, micronutrient depletion, and sleep architecture.
Primary Goal
Re-establish Agency & Embodiment
Choice in pacing. No "shoulds." Collaborative decisions. Restoration of agency is non-negotiable.

Deeper Healing Arc

Beyond routine, healing comes through:

Reclaiming agency
Restoring dignity
Creating beauty
Belonging again

Core Principles

The foundations that guide every decision in recovery.

1
Safety Before InsightThe nervous system must feel safe before it can change. Trauma is stored as threat physiology, not story.
2
Bottom-Up RegulationThe body leads; the mind follows. Breath, posture, movement, and rhythm precede cognitive processing.
3
Capacity Building, Not CatharsisExpand the window of tolerance before touching content. Capacity — not expression — predicts recovery.
4
Nervous System Flexibility Over CalmThe goal is adaptability, not constant calm. A regulated system is responsive and resilient.
5
Integration, Not ErasureRecovery doesn't mean forgetting. Memory loses its charge; identity expands beyond survival roles.
6
Energy & Resource RestorationTrauma is metabolically expensive. Food, sleep, light exposure, and pacing are therapeutic — not ancillary.
7
Relational RepairTrauma happens in relationship; healing completes there. Connection must be optional, titrated, and safe.
8
Choice, Agency & ConsentTrauma is fundamentally about loss of control. Agency is the medicine.
9
Nonlinear ProgressHealing spirals, it doesn't ascend in a straight line. Track trends, not days.
10
Gentleness Is a Strategic ChoiceSlower is often faster. Low-intensity, high-frequency inputs. Consistency over heroics.

Tier 1 — Nervous System Regulation

Goal: Reduce hypervigilance spikes and collapse states.

Daily Practice
Vagal Toning
Slow nasal breathing with extended exhales.
Daily Practice
Somatic Tracking
Gentle body awareness without trauma processing or forcing release.
Daily Practice
Consistent Sleep/Wake Schedule
Fixed anchor times within ±30 minutes every day.
Monitoring
HRV Tracking
Track trends over time — not day-to-day obsession.
Exit Criteria
Sleep consistent 80% of nights · Reduced startle reactivity · Emotional spikes shorter in duration · No frequent cognitive crashes
Timeline: ~8–16 weeks depending on severity.

Tier 1 — Metabolic & Neurochemical Repair

Goal: Stabilise cortisol and glucose variability.

Nutrition
Anti-inflammatory Protocol
Keto or low-glycemic. Stable protein intake: 1.6–2.2g/kg.
Supplementation
Key Supplements
Omega-3 repletion · Magnesium glycinate · Glycine before bed.

Tier 1 — Cognitive Baseline Restoration

Goal: Restore sustained attention without overload.

Practice
Working Memory Drills
Light drills, 5–10 minutes only.
Practice
Reading with Recall
Read a passage, then summarise from memory before moving on.
Practice
Focus Intervals
25 minutes maximum initially. No multitasking.

Tier 1 — Environmental Stabilisation

Remove chaos from the environment before adding challenge.

Space
Fixed Workspace
Same location, same setup. Predictability signals safety.
Light
Predictable Lighting
Morning sunlight. Evening warm light hygiene. Blue blockers or no screens after 7pm.
Digital
Reduced Digital Chaos
Notifications off during work. Single-tab focus. No doom-scrolling.

Tier 1 — Load Management

Strict energy budgeting. No high-intensity stress training yet.

Rule
Energy Budgeting
Treat cognitive, emotional, and physical energy as a shared finite resource.
Rule
Clear Work/Rest Boundaries
Define when work ends. Protect decompression time as non-negotiable.
Limit
No High-Intensity Training Yet
Zone-2 cardio only until nervous system stability is established.

Method

Tier 3 — Performance & Cognitive

Cognitive

Physical
Strength Training
Progressive overload with adequate recovery. Aerobic base maintenance.
Physical

Aerobic/strength training with problem-solving intervals. Lifelong learning.

Tier 3 — Identity & Integration

What to Avoid

Environments and habits that actively damage nervous system recovery.

Pattern
Chronic Unpredictability
Irregular schedules, frequent travel, shift work. Disrupts circadian rhythm and elevates baseline cortisol.
Pattern
High-Threat / High-Noise Environments
Crowds, conflict-heavy workplaces, constant alarms. Keeps amygdala hyperactive.
Pattern
Emotionally Unsafe Spaces
Hostile teams, shaming cultures, moral injury environments. Hardens trauma circuits.
Pattern
Chronic Multitasking
Reduces gray matter in anterior cingulate. Degrades working memory and error detection.
Pattern
Late-Night Screens
Suppresses melatonin, blocks memory consolidation, impairs glymphatic clearance.
Pattern
Irregular Bedtimes
Disrupts hippocampal indexing, reduces executive function, increases emotional volatility.
Pattern
Excess Stimulants
Caffeine depletes dopamine, increases anxiety, impairs sleep architecture.
Pattern
Isolation
Protects initially but prolongs trauma long-term. Connection must be optional and safe.

Monthly Reassessment

45–60 minutes once per month. Four phases.

Phase 1
Quantitative Re-Scoring
Re-score 10 domains from original audit (0–5 per item). Record this month's score, last month's score, and change.
Phase 2
Drift & Trend Analysis
Where did I improve? Where did I decline — acute or cumulative? Are there cross-domain correlations?
Phase 3
Stress Load Audit
Calculate cognitive, emotional, and physical load. Was recovery proportional to load?
Phase 4
Strategic Adjustment Plan
Choose only 2 domains to focus on next month. What will I increase? Reduce? Protect?
Immediate Intervention Required If
Sleep fragmentation >4 nights/week · Irritability increasing · Rumination increasing · Emotional numbing · Increased impulsivity · Loss of future orientation · Compulsive behaviour increasing

If 2+ present → reduce load 20–30% for 2 weeks.

Quarterly Deep Review

A comprehensive check-in to prevent long-term nervous system erosion.

Biological
Blood Markers
Inflammation markers, hormone panels, glucose variability indicators.
Physical
Body Composition
If accessible. Track trends over quarters, not weeks.
Cognitive
Cognitive Testing Benchmark
Sustained attention, working memory, executive function baseline.
Wellbeing

360-degree relational feedback. Values alignment review.
Key Principle
If performance improves but recovery speed declines, you are borrowing from the future.
